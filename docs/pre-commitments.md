# Pre-Commitments — TimkaMe v6

> Phase F deliverable. Eugene re-reads every 4 weeks. Compiled 04/18/2026.

---

These are acknowledge-reality triggers, not try-harder deadlines.

## 1. MVP timeline

**If 16 weeks from Phase 1 start, no working MVP → STOP, reassess.**

"Working MVP" = a real business owner can sign up, set up forwarding, receive AI-handled calls, get Telegram notifications, and see transcripts. Doesn't need to be polished. Must handle real calls.

What to reassess: Is the scope too large? Is 20 hrs/week not enough? Is the tech stack wrong? Is the founder's time better spent elsewhere?

## 2. Post-launch traction

**If 30 days post-launch, 0 paying customers AND <5 active prospects → 30 more days of outreach. If still zero → honest eval.**

Outreach = personal conversations, not cold email blasts. First 10 customers come from face-to-face on Brighton Beach, Russian business Facebook groups, personal network.

"Honest eval" = Is the product bad? Is the market wrong? Is the pricing wrong? Am I not doing enough outreach? Or is this just slow and needs more time?

## 3. Six-month MRR

**If 6 months post-launch, MRR <$500 (6 customers) → decide: learning project or sunset.**

$500 MRR doesn't cover costs. But if you're learning and enjoying it and can afford it, continuing is fine — just be honest that it's a learning project, not a business.

If it's a business: what needs to change to get to 20 customers?

## 4. Life changes

**If life changes (schedule change, health, family) → honest pause OK. Silent abandonment NOT OK.**

What honest pause looks like:
- Tell active customers (if any): "Service pausing on [date]. Here's how to export your data."
- Set status page to "Maintenance"
- Keep billing stopped
- Keep data available for 90 days
- Write a postmortem for yourself: what worked, what didn't, what you'd do differently

What silent abandonment looks like:
- Just stop responding to support
- Let billing run while service degrades
- Don't tell customers

The first is responsible. The second is how solo-founder SaaS gets a bad reputation.
